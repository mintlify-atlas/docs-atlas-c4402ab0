Find answers to common questions about StreamSwap, how it works, and how to use it.

## General Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is StreamSwap?">
    StreamSwap is a protocol for time-based token swaps on Cosmos blockchains. It enables projects, DAOs, and users to create streams that last for a set duration and facilitate token swaps with dynamic, community-driven pricing.
    
    Unlike traditional token sales with fixed prices, StreamSwap determines the final price based on:
    - Total amount of input tokens subscribed
    - The amount of output tokens being distributed
    - When participants enter and exit the stream
    
    This creates a fair, transparent mechanism for token distribution where the community determines the price through their participation.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How is StreamSwap different from other token sales?">
    StreamSwap differs from traditional methods in several ways:
    
    **vs. Fixed-Price ICOs:**
    - StreamSwap has no predetermined price
    - Price is determined by community participation
    - More fair price discovery mechanism
    
    **vs. Dutch Auctions (Balancer LBP):**
    - Price doesn't decrease over time
    - Participants can enter and exit during active period
    - Final price based on total participation, not time-based decay
    
    **vs. AMMs:**
    - No initial liquidity required
    - Continuous distribution over time
    - Built-in vesting and pool creation options
    
    StreamSwap provides a continuous, fair token swap mechanism with dynamic pricing.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Which blockchains does StreamSwap support?">
    StreamSwap currently supports:
    
    - **Osmosis** (osmosis-1): OSMO, ATOM, and axlUSDC as input tokens
    - **Injective** (injective-1): INJ as input token
    
    More chains and tokens will be added in future releases. The protocol is built on CosmWasm and can be deployed to any Cosmos SDK chain that supports it.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Is StreamSwap open source?">
    Yes! StreamSwap is fully open source under the Apache 2.0 license.
    
    - **GitHub**: [StreamSwapProtocol/streamswap-contracts](https://github.com/StreamSwapProtocol/streamswap-contracts)
    - **License**: Apache 2.0
    - **Contributions**: Community contributions are welcome
    
    You can review all the code, run your own tests, and even deploy your own instances of the contracts.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Stream Mechanics

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How does pricing work?">
    StreamSwap uses dynamic pricing determined by participation:
    
    **Average Price Formula:**
    ```
    Average Price = Total Input Spent / (Output Supply - Output Remaining)
    ```
    
    **Key Points:**
    - Price is not known until the stream ends
    - More participation = higher price for the output token
    - Less participation = lower price for the output token
    - You receive tokens proportional to your share of total spending
    
    **Example:**
    - Stream offers 1,000,000 PROJECT tokens
    - 100,000 OSMO is subscribed by all participants
    - Average price = 100,000 / 1,000,000 = 0.1 OSMO per PROJECT token
    - If you spent 1,000 OSMO, you'd receive 10,000 PROJECT tokens
    
    The earlier you subscribe, the more of the distribution period you participate in, which affects your final token allocation.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What are the different stream states?">
    A StreamSwap stream goes through several states:
    
    1. **Waiting**: Stream created but not yet active. No interactions allowed.
    
    2. **Bootstrapping**: Participants can subscribe, but no spending occurs yet. You can withdraw your full amount during this phase.
    
    3. **Active**: The stream is live. Tokens are distributed continuously based on subscriptions. You can still withdraw unspent tokens.
    
    4. **Ended**: Distribution complete. Participants must call `exit` to claim their tokens and refund unspent input tokens.
    
    5. **Finalized**: Creator has finalized the stream. Vesting and liquidity pools (if configured) are set up.
    
    6. **Cancelled**: Stream didn't meet threshold requirements. All participants can withdraw their input tokens, creator can reclaim output tokens.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What is the threshold, and what happens if it's not met?">
    The **threshold** is a minimum amount of input tokens that must be subscribed for the stream to proceed.
    
    **If Threshold is Met:**
    - Stream proceeds normally through all states
    - Tokens are distributed to participants
    - Creator receives the input tokens
    
    **If Threshold is NOT Met:**
    - Stream is cancelled
    - All participants can withdraw their full subscription amount
    - Creator can reclaim all output tokens
    - No fees are applied (except transaction fees)
    
    Thresholds protect creators from distributing tokens if there's insufficient interest, and protect participants from receiving tokens at potentially unfavorable prices due to low participation.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How is my token allocation calculated?">
    Your token allocation depends on:
    
    1. **Amount**: How much input token you subscribed
    2. **Time**: When you subscribed and how long you stayed in
    3. **Total Participation**: How much others subscribed
    
    **Share Calculation:**
    - When you subscribe, you receive "shares" based on your amount
    - Shares represent your portion of the total spending pool
    - During distribution, tokens are allocated based on share percentage
    
    **Time Factor:**
    - The longer your tokens are in the stream, the more of the distribution you participate in
    - Early subscribers who stay through the end receive more tokens than late subscribers with the same amount
    
    **Example:**
    - Stream runs for 10 days
    - You subscribe 1,000 OSMO on day 0
    - Someone else subscribes 1,000 OSMO on day 5
    - You'll receive more tokens because you participated in the full distribution period
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Participating in Streams

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I subscribe to a stream?">
    To subscribe to a stream:
    
    1. Find an active stream (in Waiting, Bootstrapping, or Active state)
    2. Decide how much input token you want to commit
    3. Send a `Subscribe` transaction with your tokens
    4. Your tokens are locked, and you receive shares in the stream
    
    **Important:**
    - Subscriptions are possible during Bootstrapping and Active states
    - You cannot subscribe to Ended, Finalized, or Cancelled streams
    - Make sure you have enough tokens plus transaction fees
    - Review stream parameters before subscribing (duration, threshold, etc.)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I withdraw my tokens early?">
    Yes, you can withdraw tokens during certain phases:
    
    **Bootstrapping Phase:**
    - Withdraw your full amount anytime
    - No tokens have been spent yet
    - No penalties or fees
    
    **Active Phase:**
    - Withdraw only unspent tokens
    - Already-spent tokens cannot be recovered
    - Your shares are reduced proportionally
    
    **After Stream Ends:**
    - Use `exit` instead of `withdraw`
    - Claim your distributed output tokens
    - Receive refund of any unspent input tokens
    
    To withdraw, send a `Withdraw` message specifying the amount. The contract will calculate how much you can withdraw based on current spending.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="When can I exit a stream?">
    You must explicitly exit a stream after it ends to claim your tokens:
    
    **When to Exit:**
    - Stream state is "Ended" or "Finalized"
    - You've participated in the stream (subscribed tokens)
    - You haven't already exited
    
    **What Happens on Exit:**
    - Receive your share of distributed output tokens
    - Receive refund of any unspent input tokens
    - Exit fee is deducted (if configured)
    - Your position is closed
    
    **Important:**
    - Exiting is not automatic - you must call the `exit` function
    - Exit fees (if any) are applied to the output tokens
    - You can check your position at any time to see your claimable amounts
    
    Set reminders to exit after a stream ends to claim your tokens!
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What fees does StreamSwap charge?">
    StreamSwap has several types of fees:
    
    **Creation Fee:**
    - Paid by stream creators when creating a new stream
    - Helps prevent spam and funds protocol development
    - Collected and managed by DAO governance
    
    **Exit Fee:**
    - Optional fee configured per stream
    - Applied to output tokens when participants exit
    - Set by stream creator (can be 0)
    - Collected by the stream creator or treasury
    
    **Transaction Fees:**
    - Standard blockchain gas fees
    - Paid to validators, not StreamSwap
    - Varies by network and transaction complexity
    
    **No Subscription Fees:**
    - Subscribing to a stream has no protocol fee
    - Only pay blockchain transaction fees
    
    Fee amounts are controlled by DAO governance and can be adjusted through governance proposals.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Creating Streams

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I create a stream?">
    To create a stream:
    
    1. **Choose Network & Token**: Select which controller contract (network and input token)
    2. **Prepare Output Tokens**: Have your output tokens ready to deposit
    3. **Define Parameters**:
       - Stream name and description
       - Start and end times
       - Threshold amount
       - Exit fee (if any)
       - Vesting or pool parameters (optional)
    4. **Submit Transaction**: Send `CreateStream` with your output tokens and creation fee
    5. **Receive Stream Address**: Contract instantiates your stream and returns the address
    
    For detailed instructions, see the [Creating Streams guide](/guides/creating-streams).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What parameters do I need to set when creating a stream?">
    Key parameters when creating a stream:
    
    **Required:**
    - **Output Token**: The token you're distributing (denom and amount)
    - **Start Time**: When the stream becomes active
    - **End Time**: When distribution ends
    - **Threshold**: Minimum input tokens required to proceed
    
    **Optional:**
    - **Exit Fee**: Fee percentage taken on exit (0-100%)
    - **Bootstrapping Duration**: Time for subscriptions before spending starts
    - **Vesting Parameters**: Lock distributed tokens with vesting schedule
    - **Pool Parameters**: Automatically create liquidity pool after stream
    - **URL/Description**: Metadata about your stream
    
    Choose parameters carefully - they cannot be changed after creation!
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can I cancel my stream?">
    Streams are automatically cancelled if threshold requirements aren't met. As a creator, you cannot manually cancel a stream once it's created (to protect participants).
    
    **Cancellation Scenarios:**
    - Threshold not met by end of bootstrapping phase
    - Stream never reaches active state due to low participation
    
    **After Cancellation:**
    - You can call `finalize` to reclaim your output tokens
    - Participants can withdraw their full input tokens
    - No fees are applied (except transaction fees)
    
    To ensure your stream proceeds, set a realistic threshold based on expected interest.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What happens after my stream ends?">
    After your stream ends:
    
    1. **Ended State**: Stream stops distributing tokens, participants can exit
    2. **Finalize**: You (creator) can call `finalize` to:
       - Collect all input tokens (minus exit fees if configured)
       - Set up vesting contracts (if configured)
       - Create liquidity pools (if configured)
       - Stream transitions to "Finalized" state
    3. **Withdraw**: You can withdraw any undistributed output tokens
    
    **Important:**
    - You must call `finalize` to collect your input tokens
    - Participants must call `exit` to claim their output tokens
    - Neither happens automatically
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
